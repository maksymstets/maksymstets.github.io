Who am I?

My name is Maksym Stets and I'm a driven PhD researcher leveraging a deep background in biotech and molecular sciences to transition into a career focused on bioinformatics and data-driven biology. I'm passionate about using computational tools to solve biological problems and advance biotechnology.

Short bio

I recently completed my Master's degree in Biology with a specialization in Biochemistry from Ivan Franko National University of Lviv in December 2022. Throughout my undergraduate and graduate studies, I specialized in molecular biology and gained a comprehensive understanding of biochemical processes, particularly in yeast genetics and protein degradation mechanisms.

Currently, I am pursuing my PhD at Lviv Polytechnic National University's Institute of Chemistry and Chemical Technologies, focusing on Biotechnologies and Bioengineering (expected completion: September 2027).

During my academic journey, I have gained extensive laboratory experience including yeast and bacteria culturing, DNA and protein isolation, PCR, Western blotting, gel electrophoresis, and enzyme activity assays. My professional experience includes work at PRJSC ENZYM COMPANY, where I developed new biotechnology products, managed pilot line operations, and worked on fermentation processes and enzymatic hydrolysis.

I have also expanded my skills in bioinformatics and computational biology through courses including Harvard's CS50 Introduction to Programming with Python (January 2026), Introduction to R and RNA Sequencing from LifeSciences Course (April 2025), and Introduction to Industrial Bioprocess Development from Technical University of Denmark via Coursera (February 2024). My main interests are RNA sequencing analysis, data analysis using Python and R, and applying computational methods to biotechnology and bioengineering challenges.

PhD Research Project (Current)

Biotechnologies and Bioengineering

Currently pursuing doctoral research at Lviv Polytechnic National University, Institute of Chemistry and Chemical Technologies, focusing on advanced biotechnology applications and bioengineering processes.

Master's Thesis (2022)

Identification of genes involved in degradation of cytosol proteins in methylotrophic yeast Komagataella phaffii

This research focused on understanding the genetic mechanisms underlying protein degradation in the industrially important yeast species Komagataella phaffii, which has significant applications in biotechnology and recombinant protein production.

Bachelor's Thesis (2021)

Obtaining and analysis of recombinant strains Komagataella phaffii yeast with disrupted degradation of cytoplasmic proteins
